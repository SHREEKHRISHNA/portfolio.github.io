Design Components in Fusion 360

  • Bodies

    Bodies represent the physical geometry of your design. Each body can be a distinct part or feature, such as a main structure, a housing, or a component within a larger assembly.

  • Components

    Components are individual parts within your design that can be moved, assembled, and modified independently. They allow you to organize and manage complex assemblies with multiple interconnected parts.

  • Features

    Features refer to the specific operations or modifications applied to bodies or components to create or edit their geometry. This includes extrusions, cuts, fillets, chamfers, and other modeling operations.

  • Constraints

    Constraints define the relationships between different components or bodies within an assembly. They ensure proper alignment, motion, and interaction between parts, such as mates, joints, and motion links.

  • Sketches

    Sketches serve as the foundation for creating 3D geometry in Fusion 360. They consist of 2D profiles drawn on reference planes or surfaces, which can be used to define the shape and dimensions of features.

  • Lofts

    Lofts are a type of feature that creates a smooth transition between two or more profiles. They are commonly used to create complex shapes, surfaces, or transitions between different sections of a design.

  • Patterns

    Patterns allow you to replicate features or components in a design across specified directions and distances. This includes linear patterns, circular patterns, and component patterns, which can streamline the modeling process for repetitive elements.

  • Assemblies

    Assemblies are collections of components and bodies arranged in a hierarchical structure to represent the physical relationships between parts. They enable you to model complex systems or products composed of multiple interconnected parts.

  • Sculpting Features

    Sculpting features provide advanced modeling tools for creating organic shapes, freeform surfaces, and artistic designs. These include sculpting brushes, T-splines, and deformation tools for shaping and refining geometry.

  • Sheet Metal Features

    For designs involving sheet metal fabrication, Fusion 360 offers specialized features for creating and modifying sheet metal components. This includes flanges, bends, hems, and other operations specific to sheet metal design.

Piston and Crank Shaft

1]Sketching: Begin by sketching the profile of the piston and the shape of the crankshaft.
2] Extruding: Extrude the sketches to create 3D models of the piston and crankshaft.
3] Adding Details: Add features such as piston rings, connecting rod mounts, and fillets to the piston. Design the crankshaft with journals, counterweights, and fillets for strength.
4] Assembly: Create an assembly file and insert the piston and crankshaft components. Use joints to simulate the motion between the two components.
5] Testing and Refinement: Test the motion of the piston and crankshaft assembly. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure smooth operation.
6] Documentation: Finally, create drawings and documentation to communicate the design intent and specifications of the piston and crankshaft assembly.

knuckle joint

2] Extrude the sketch to create a 3D model of the joint.
3] Add fillets and chamfers to smooth edges and enhance durability.
4] Assemble the knuckle joint with other components using Fusion 360's assembly tools.

Diamond

1]Draw a Triangle: Use the Line or Polygon tool to draw a triangle. You can specify the dimensions and angles to create an equilateral triangle if desired.
2] Add Dimensions and Constraints: Add dimensions and constraints to fully define the size and shape of your triangle. This ensures that it remains symmetrical and proportional.
